DCU Orchestra

Brand identity, creative direction, logo design, digital and print assets, social media content, and event branding.

01.Overview

As the Public Relations Officer I created a full brand identity and content system for DCU Orchestra to be used throughout the academic year. The society welcomes all students and alumni who play an instrument and want to perform. My goal is to increase the society’s online and on-campus visibility, build a strong and welcoming visual identity that attracts members, and make joining and staying involved easier and more appealing. The materials I designed will be used on Instagram and Linkedin, and in print for on-campus promotions.

02.Brand Identity

Logos

Our logo system blends music and college pride, with a crotchet note replacing the “D” in “DCU Orchestra”. Using all lowercase gives the design a friendly, student-led feel. Simplified versions ensure clarity at any size or platform.

Colour scheme

I chose blue and gold as the main brand colours to reflect the orchestra’s mix of tradition and energy. Blue symbolises structure and unity, while gold represents creativity and excitement. These colours tie into DCU’s identity and stand out across print and digital formats.

Typography

I picked the fonts Lora, a classic serif, and Poppins, a clean sans-serif, to balance formality with approachability.

I designed a set of social media templates to keep our posts clear, consistent and easy to recognise. The system includes three main types: information, announcements and engagement (photo-based). Each type uses its own colour — blue for announcements, white for information and gold for engagement - to maintain consistency and recognisability.

Instagram highlight covers

I created highlight covers to organise the most important posts at the top of our profile by year. The gradually deepening colours represent the society’s growth over time.

Banners

I’m designing visual assets to encourage sign-ups for DCU’s upcoming Clubs & Societies Fair. These include posters, stickers, and bookmarks with a custom QR code linking directly to the membership page, making it easy for students to join on the spot while keeping the brand visible throughout the year. To decorate the stand, I created table tent cards and banners. A phone graphic, inspired by the iPhone QR scanning interface, reinforces DCU Orchestra’s online presence, while an envelope design symbolises an invitation to join the orchestra. One poster features a music staff with notes on D and C as a nod to DCU. For the “U,” I used a quaver rest which is a short pause that invites new members to complete the melody by joining and playing their part.
