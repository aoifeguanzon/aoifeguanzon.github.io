DCU Orchestra

Brand identity development, creative direction, logo design, print assets, social content, post templates, event branding and promotional materials.

01.Overview

As the Public Relations Officer I'm in the process of creating a full brand identity and content system for DCU Orchestra to be used throughout the academic year. The society welcomes all students and alumni who play an instrument and want to perform. These materials are designed for Instagram and Linkedin as well as printed promotions around campus.

02.Goals

  1. Increase the society’s online and on-campus visibility.
  2. Build a strong, welcoming visual identity that attracts both students and alumni.
  3. Make joining and staying involved with the society easier and more appealing.

03.Branding System

Colour scheme

I chose cobalt blue and gold as the main brand colours to reflect the orchestra’s mix of tradition and energy; blue for structure and unity with gold for creativity and excitement. These colours also tie into DCU’s visual identity and stand out across print and digital formats.

Typography

I picked the fonts Lora, a classic serif, and Poppins, a clean sans-serif, to balance our orchestra’s formal style with a friendly, welcoming tone.

The logo system blends music and college pride. A crotchet note replaces the “d” in “DCU Orchestra,” grounding the logo in musical identity. Using all lowercase gives the design a friendly, student-led tone. Simplified versions ensure the logo remains clear at any size or platform.

I designed a set of social media templates to keep the DCU Orchestra’s posts clear, consistent and easy to recognise. The system includes three main types: information, announcements and engagement. Each type uses its own colour — blue for announcements, white for information and gold for engagement — to reflect DCU’s identity and help posts stand out.

Instagram highlight covers

To make our Instagram page easier to navigate, I organised our most important posts into yearly highlights at the top of our Instagram profile. The gradually deepening colours symbolise the society’s growth over time.

05.Event Branding

Clubs & societies fair

I designed visual assets to drive signups during DCU’s Clubs & Societies Fair. These include posters, stickers, and bookmarks featuring a custom QR code linking directly to the membership page. The practical formats make it easy for students to join on the spot and keep the brand visible throughout the year.

To decorate our stand I made table tent cards and banners. A phone graphic was inspired by the real iPhone QR scanning interface to reinforce our online presence, while an envelope design symbolises our invitation to join the orchestra.

One of the designs has a music staff with notes on D and C as a nod to DCU. For the 'U', I used a quarter rest; a short pause that invites others to complete the melody by joining the orchestra and playing their part.
